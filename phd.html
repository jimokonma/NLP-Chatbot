M.Phil./Ph.D.

General Regulations

REQUIREMENTS

Candidates who, having satisfied all requirements for the Degree of Master of Science in Industrial and Production Engineering University of Ibadan (or other Universities approved by Senate), and are eligible, could be recommended to proceed to the degree of M.Phil. In this respect, the guidelines of Postgraduate School regulations apply.

MASTER OF PHILOSOPHY (M.Phil.)

OBJECTIVES

The aim of this programme Master of Philosophy (M.Phil.) in Industrial and Production Engineering is to produce graduates capable of carrying out research of high calibre. Such graduates should also be sufficiently prepared to pursue studies leading to a Ph.D. in Industrial and Production Engineering if they so wish. Furthermore, they should be capable of supplying relevant advanced skills and technology in Industrial and Production Engineering systems.

DURATION OF STUDY

Full Time: The programme shall normally last four (4) semesters after initial registration for full-time postgraduate candidates with relevant MSc degrees.

Part Time: Part-time candidates will take, at least, six (6) semesters from initial registration to complete the degree.

In both cases, the guidelines of Postgraduate School apply.

DOCTOR OF PHILOSOPHY (Ph.D.)

OBJECTIVES

The objective of this programme is to produce graduates capable of carrying out research of high calibre and/or wishing to teach at tertiary institutions and to demonstrate capability to lead research projects, programmes and groups. Furthermore, graduates from this programme should be capable of supplying relevant skills to Industrial and Production Engineering systems.

DURATION OF STUDY

Full Time: The programme shall normally last not less than six (6) semesters from the date of first registration for Ph.D. degree in the case of full-time registration.

Part Time: Part-time candidates will take not less than eight (8) semesters from initial registration to complete the degree.

In both cases, the guidelines of Postgraduate School apply.

CONDITION FOR AWARD OF DEGREE

Candidates may be required to take advanced courses at the 800 level. The 800-level course should not exceed 18 units and shall normally include (but may not be limited to) advanced methodology, data management and research proposal writing. The Department may also prescribe some 700 level courses if necessary. However, the programme structure shall be such as to allow students to undertake courses and research concurrently and graduate within the minimum stipulated duration.

PROGRAMME STRUCTURE

The M.Phil. and Ph.D. programmes are structured such that candidates have the choice to pursue any of the following options.

  1. Ergonomics/Human Factors Engineering
  2. Production/Manufacturing Engineering
  3. Operations Research
  4. Operations Research
  5. Systems Engineering
  1. Ergonomics/Human Factors Engineering:

    This is concerned with the design of work systems with emphasis on the relation of Man to his working environment. It includes such subjects as Methods Engineering, Safety Engineering, Biomechanics and Anthropometrics. Others are the study of human capabilities/limitations, human energy requirements, controls and environmental work factors.

  2. Production/Manufacturing Engineering:

    This is the area that deals with the analysis and design of production system. It covers such subjects as process design, operations design, design of material handling mechanisms, tooling, and facilities layout including robotics, automation, Computer Aided Design and manufacturing, flexible and lean manufacturing systems. Others are design of production inventory and quality control systems.

  3. Operations Research:

    Operations Research (OR) combines mathematical modelling with the principles of optimization to analyse and design new or modify existing operations. The main purpose is to improve performance of all aspects of work systems. It includes such subjects as linear programming, non-linear programming, multi-criteria decision-making engineering statistics, game theory, queuing theory, evolutionary algorithms etc.

  4. Engineering Management

    Engineering Management is the use of scientific techniques in organizing, planning and controlling large-scale operations. The subjects included are engineering economy, costing and cost control, decision theory, technological forecasting, project planning and control, team management, etc.

  5. Systems Engineering

    This is concerned with the applications of computers, mathematical and simulation modelling techniques to the design analysis, synthesis and control of complex systems. The subjects covered are design of information systems, computers and computer communication techniques, simulation control theory, etc.
